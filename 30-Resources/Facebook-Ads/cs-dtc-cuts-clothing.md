# Cuts Clothing — Signal-Dense Creative + Retargeting Migration

## Key Metrics
- **ROAS:** 2.9 → 4.8x (65% improvement)
- **CPA:** $48 → $34 (-29% reduction)
- **Timeframe:** Q1 2026, migration from broad to signal-dense creative strategy

## Strategy Breakdown

### The Creative Problem
Cuts was running low-quality video ads optimized for views rather than conversions. High view counts with low signal density — viewers spent <2 seconds on screen, not engaging with the product or CTA. This sent weak signals to Meta's algorithm about who converted.

### Signal-Dense Creative Framework
1. **First 3 seconds:** Face + product in frame simultaneously (not just face)
2. **Middle section:** Real transformation/use case shown within first 8 seconds
3. **Final 5 seconds:** Clear CTA overlay with offer — no fade-to-black, no "link in bio"

### Creative Testing Sequence
1. ABO testing: 8 creative concepts × 2 formats each (UGc + polished)
2. Winner selection after 7 days, $50/adset testing budget minimum
3. Migrate winners to CBO with cost cap at target CPA
4. Weekly creative refresh every 14 days

### Retargeting Layer
- Site visitors (>30s) → Dynamic product ads with top-reviewed items
- Add-to-cart no purchase (14-day window) → Carousel of abandoned + similar products
- Purchase history → Upsell campaigns for complementary products, exclude recent buyers <7d

### Key Takeaway
For DTC clothing brands, **creative quality beats targeting sophistication** going into 2026. Signal-dense creative — where the product is visible early, use case is shown quickly, and CTA is unambiguous — generates stronger algorithmic signals than any interest or lookalike combination. Meta's Advantage+ with signal-dense inputs became the dominant lever for scale.
