# High-Ticket Home Improvement — ROAS 1.18 → 6.47x Scale-Out (Q1 2026)

## Key Metrics
- **ROAS:** 1.18 → 6.47x (548% improvement)
- **Average ticket:** $12,000+ per job (roofing, siding, windows)
- **Timeframe:** Q1 2026, full service-area expansion strategy

## Strategy Breakdown

### The High-Ticket Challenge
Home improvement campaigns struggle with high CPMs in large markets ($25-40/1000 impressions for roofing), creating thin ROAS margins on first pass. The key is increasing conversion value while maintaining lead quality.

### Service-Area Expansion Methodology
1. **Start narrow:** Launch in one city or metro area with highest local demand + lowest competition
2. **Radius targeting + language optimization** to match service territory
3. **Prove ROAS > 3x** before expanding service radius by 25% increments
4. **Creative localization:** Different ad variants per city highlighting local landmarks, weather-specific needs

### Lead Form Optimization
- Long-form forms with custom qualifying questions: budget range ($10K-$25K), timeline (30/60/90 days)
- Exclusion logic: "Do you own your home?" → No = skip lead
- Follow-up within 15 minutes triggers 4x higher show rate

### Scaling to 6.47x ROAS
The critical insight: high-ticket services thrive on **consolidated messaging with strong proof**. Rather than testing dozens of angles, this campaign ran ONE message — "Trusted by {local city} homeowners for {X years}" — with visual proof (before/after slider) and 3 verified reviews in the ad. The simplicity allowed Meta's algorithm to optimize faster across a larger audience pool without creative noise.

### Key Takeaway
For high-ticket local services, **message clarity + proof density > audience sophistication**. A single well-crafted ad speaking to one specific problem (e.g., "Roof leaks during spring storms") with strong social proof consistently outperformed multi-concept campaigns. Scale comes from geographic expansion, not creative fragmentation.
